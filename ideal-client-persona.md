# Ideal Client Persona — Charlotte Lloyd

## Sarah Chen — 41, Leadership Coach

**Location**: Austin, TX (moved from San Francisco during the pandemic)

**Family**: Married to David (software engineer turned startup CTO), two kids aged 7 and 9

**Background**: 15 years in HR leadership at Meta and Stripe. Left corporate 18 months ago to launch her own leadership coaching practice. She helps mid-career women in tech navigate the jump from senior manager to director.

**Revenue**: Her coaching business does ~$8K/month. She needs $20K/month to match her old base salary. She's been stuck at this ceiling for 6 months.

**Education**: MBA from USC, certified by ICF (PCC level)

---

### The Story

Sarah left Meta in early 2024 with a solid network, a niche she believed in, and 18 months of savings. The first 6 months were a slow burn — word-of-mouth clients from former colleagues, a few referrals. She hit $7-8K/month and hit a wall.

She has 4,200 followers on LinkedIn. She posts twice a week on leadership topics. She gets likes and comments from other coaches, not from her actual target buyers (senior ICs and new managers at SaaS companies). Her DMs rarely turn into sales calls. When they do, prospects ghost after the first conversation.

She tried a VA to handle outreach. It generated meetings but none of them converted — they felt generic. She tried posting more frequently. She tried lead magnets. She spent $500 on ads and got 2 leads that went nowhere.

She has the credibility (Meta, Stripe, ICF certification) and the offer (a 12-week leadership accelerator for new directors). What she doesn't have is a reliable client acquisition system.

She's frustrated because she knows she's good at coaching. The gap isn't her delivery — it's her pipeline. She needs someone to show her exactly how to turn LinkedIn conversations into $5K+ enrollments, consistently.

### Why Charlotte Specifically

Sarah needs a guide who has been in the trenches — someone who actually sold in high-ticket B2B, not a social media guru who preaches "post daily and the algorithm will reward you."

Charlotte's message resonates because:
- Charlotte doesn't pretend a big following equals sales (Sarah has 4,200 followers, not 100K)
- Charlotte focuses on the DM-to-call-to-close pipeline, which is exactly where Sarah is leaking deals
- Charlotte's "buyers decide before the call" framework shifts Sarah's attention from content-perfection to conversation-quality
- Charlotte has closed $20M+ in B2B — she's not theoretical
- Charlotte started her business at 43, which speaks to Sarah's timeline concerns

### Objections She Initially Had

- "I've already tried LinkedIn outreach and it felt spammy"
- "Can a salesperson really teach a coach how to sell? Coaching is relational, not transactional"
- "I don't want to be pushy or aggressive"
- "Is this going to be another course, or actual hand-holding?"

### The Moment She Decided to Work With Charlotte

Sarah was scrolling LinkedIn and saw Charlotte's post: *"Your buyer isn't ignoring your DMs. They're protecting themselves from another pitch disguised as a conversation."*

She clicked through to Charlotte's profile. Read 5 posts. Watched the "How to Get Clients on LinkedIn" podcast clip. She realised her problem wasn't that she was bad at sales — it was that she was approaching the conversation from the wrong place. She booked a call that same day.
