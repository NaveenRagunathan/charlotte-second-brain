I thought 23 years in sales would make entrepreneurship easier.
 

It didn't.
 
Yesterday I was speaking at WTW about high-trust deal making.
 
It reminded me of one of the biggest lessons I've learned since leaving corporate.
 
I already knew how to sell.
I knew how to negotiate.
Handle objections.
Close complex deals.
 
What I underestimated was everything that happens before the sales conversation.
 
Building a business on LinkedIn taught me that buyers don't start deciding on the call.
 
They start deciding the moment they discover you.
 
Your content.
Your positioning.
Your reputation.
 
The conversations people have about you when you're not in the room.
 
By the time someone books a call, they've usually formed an opinion.
 
The call doesn't create trust.
It confirms it.
 
That's why becoming better at sales wasn't the biggest shift in my business.
 
Building trust before the conversation was.
 
It's also why I built my business on LinkedIn.
 
Because it's one of the best places to earn trust before anyone ever becomes a client.
 
Looking back, that's the lesson I wish I'd learned years earlier.

But it was never too late to start....
 
The best sales calls don't create demand.
They convert the trust you've already built.
