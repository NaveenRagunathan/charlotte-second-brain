The moment a buyer asks:
“How soon will I see results?”
Most founders answer it wrong.

Not from inexperience. More the fact they’re nervous.

In that moment, the deal is already leaning one way.
It sounds like a timeline question.

It isn’t.
It’s a risk question.
When someone asks about speed, they’re really asking:

“Is this worth betting on?” 
“Do I trust you?” 
“Do I trust myself?”

If you answer with: “It depends.” “Everyone’s different.”
You’ve stayed surface level.
Instead say:

❓“That’s a fair question. Before I answer, what does ‘results’ mean to you?”

🟣 Because for one buyer, results mean revenue.
🟣 For another, it means pipeline movement.
🟣 For another, it means confidence in direction.

If you don’t define it together, you’re solving the wrong problem. Then anchor it.

🌶️ “Clients at your stage usually see X first. 
Revenue follows Y. But only when Z is implemented.”

Now you’re not promising.
You’re demonstrating pattern recognition.

Just remember this: 
You don’t control the outcome.
You control the guidance.

Serious buyers don’t want guarantees. They want leadership. Handle this moment well, and you don’t reassure.

You reposition yourself.
