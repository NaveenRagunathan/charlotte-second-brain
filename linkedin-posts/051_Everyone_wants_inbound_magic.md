Everyone wants inbound magic. 
Few want to master the middle.
 
We’re in a season where “content that sells for you” is the promise.
 
I see it with founders and consultants every week.
 
The desire is real. The content is there. The leads exist.
 
But when you zoom in, the magic breaks in the middle:
 
Leads that say “this looks amazing” and never book. 
DMs that turn into free consulting, not decisions. 
Calls that end with “let me think about it.” 
Follow-up that stops after one polite nudge.
 
And still, the answer everyone sells you is: 
post more, post louder, post everywhere.
 
The part no one markets:
 
Inbound only works when the middle of your sales process is tight. 
That’s where revenue is quietly leaking:
 
→ How you qualify before the call 
→ How you run the conversation 
→ How you handle objections 
→ How you follow up without begging 
→ How you hold your pricing when it gets uncomfortable
 
When you master the middle, content doesn’t have to be magic. 
It just has to be consistent.
 
The result? Fewer leads. Cleaner pipeline. 
Higher close rate. Calmer nervous system.
 
So if you’re posting your heart out and still not fully booked, you don’t need more reach.
 
You need to fix the middle.
 
What’s the “messy middle” you’ve been avoiding?
