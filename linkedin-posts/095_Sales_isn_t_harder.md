Sales isn’t harder. 

You’re just using an outdated playbook.

I spent 23 years in sales.

Closed deals with Danone, PepsiCo, Kraft Foods… 
Even the Presidencies of Brazil, Canada, and Mozambique.

Five-figure deals? Signed. No procurement. No legal. 

One decision-maker. 
One call. 
Deal done.

But that was then.
Today?

→ Buyers are more informed. 
→ There’s a committee behind every yes. 
→ And you’re not the only expert in the room.

The old way of selling? Not built for the modern buyer.

That’s why so many coaches and consultants are stuck.

→ They’re winging sales calls. 
→ They’re still trying to “handle objections.” 
→ They’re selling like it’s 2010 — not 2025.

Sales isn’t about pressure. It’s about clarity. 
Your process needs to match the buyer’s complexity. 
And confidence only converts when it’s paired with structure.

Sales has never been easy. Not then. Not now. Not ever.
But it 𝘪𝘴 learnable. And the people winning today?
They don’t romanticize how it used to work. 

They evolve. 
They refine. 
They adapt to the buyer in front of them.

Work hard on the right things.

📌 The market’s changed. 
Your sales approach should too.
