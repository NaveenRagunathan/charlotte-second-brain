Losing my mum at 17 shifted my entire life. 

It really shaped me. 

Most successful people have an “aha” moment. 
Mine didn't come from a job or going to uni. 
It was sitting in a hospital room, watching her fade… 

Even then, she asked me: “What’s your vision?” 

My mum believed I could build something meaningful. 
And, I believed her. 

So I did… I stopped playing by other people’s rules. 
I was supposed to study law. 
But I went into sales instead. 

Selling taught me what no MBA ever could: 
• How to handle rejection without faltering 
• How to rebuild when everything falls apart. 
• How to find opportunity in the word "no.” 

• Every hang-up was training for boardroom rejections 
• Every difficult prospect prepared me for negotiations 
• Every door slammed taught me to find another way in 

Your competitive advantage isn’t your education. 
It’s your ability to get through the s**t times. 

It’s keeping going when others quit. 
Grit isn't given, it's earned.

Everything I achieved started with losing my mum. 
When you have nothing left to lose
You have everything to gain. 

So when you get to a new level in business, 
Remember what got you there in the first place.
