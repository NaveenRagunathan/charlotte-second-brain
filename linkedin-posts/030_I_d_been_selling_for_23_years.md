I’d been selling for 23 years.
But I didn’t feel like a real business owner until Dubai.

It wasn't the revenue.
It was the actual room.

A serious stage. 
A serious client. 
People expecting leadership.

Up until then, it was: 
Late nights. Stolen weekends. 
A “side hustle.” Me proving this could work.

At 43, I built it in corporate. 
At 45, I left corporate. 
At 48, it pays my old yearly salary in a month.

But Dubai was the big change.
(Training in July + August = air con saved me)

That was the moment I stopped “trying” and started building.

No one talks about this part:
The hardest leap isn’t quitting. 
It’s believing you BELONG in bigger rooms.

Sales isn’t glamorous. 
It’s uncomfortable. It stretches you. 
It exposes every insecurity you’ve been managing.

But it also opens doors that safe careers never will.

That trip wasn’t just business. It was identity.
Proof that reinvention has no expiry date.

International Women’s Day is great. 
But we don’t need one day to validate women building, leading, selling, scaling. 

That work happens in July heat in Dubai. 
In rooms where you’re the only woman at the table. 

Reinvention doesn’t have an expiry date. 
And ambition doesn’t need permission. 

If you’re building after 40, you’re not late. 
You’re layered. 😀
