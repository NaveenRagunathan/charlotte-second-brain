My son closed me on getting a puppy and I didn’t even see it coming.

The other day, he asked for a dog.

Not just any dog, the one my friend has.
(The cute dachshund in the photo)

I said no.

But he didn’t stop.

First, he hit me with logic: 
“Mom, I’ll take care of it. I’ll feed it, walk it—everything.”

Then, urgency: 
“What if someone else adopts him first?”

And finally, emotion: 
“I’ll remember this forever if we bring him home.”

And just like that - I caved.

Not because he begged.
Not because he wore me down.

But because he tapped into what actually mattered to me:

I want him to learn responsibility.
I don’t want to regret saying no to sthg meaningful.
I love the idea of a dog bringing joy to our home.
I want to create lasting memories.

And that’s exactly how sales works.

People don’t buy because of 
logic, urgency, or persuasion tactics.

They buy because something about your offer 
SPEAKS to what they TRULY want.

The best business owners don’t convince.
They listen. They understand. 
They meet people where they are.

Want more clients?
Stop selling features. 
Start speaking to what actually MOVES them.

Because when your offer aligns with what your ideal client really wants
THEY SELL themselves on working with you.
