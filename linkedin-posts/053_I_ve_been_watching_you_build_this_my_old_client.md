'I've been watching you build this,' my old client said in Madrid.
We hadn't spoken in 8 years or more.

Back then, I was the one flying to them.
(Well, met one of them in South Africa). 

Charleston, South Carolina and Karen.
Working with their regional development team.
Helping them attract international investment.

Conference rooms. Business cards. Formal follow-up emails.
And some zoom calls. 

That was 2016 and 2020. 
Before I'd even heard the term "personal branding."

In October in Madrid, same people, same warmth.
Completely different conversation.

"I've been following your journey," Brian said.

"Watched you leave corporate, build your business, share what you were learning."

We'd never really interacted on LinkedIn.
But he'd been there.

Karen and I connected when she became a client.
I prospected to her via LinkedIn.

Reading. Watching. Understanding who I'd become and what I now help people do.

The meetings I used to chase now come to me.

The credibility I used to establish over multiple touchpoints gets built before we ever speak.

The "are you the right person for this" questions get answered by months of posts they've already read.

Your content is selling you to people you haven't met yet.

And it's re-selling you to people who knew you before.

That's it. That's the whole game.
