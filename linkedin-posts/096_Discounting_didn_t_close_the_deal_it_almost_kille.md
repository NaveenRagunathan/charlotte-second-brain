Discounting didn’t close the deal, it almost killed it.

The $70K lesson I had to learn the hard way.

Desperate to hit my numbers back in corporate.
I offered a 5% discount to get a deal closed.
The prospect’s response?

“We’ll need to amend our PO to reflect the NEW value."
I had $70K approved.

How was I going to explain this change to my boss?

That’s when it hit me hard: 

I'd ASSUMED the discount would close the deal.
When it was already coming in.

I let my:
• Desperation and urgency 
• Create a problem that didn’t exist.

I learned the hard way: Assumptions cost you. 

Whether it’s money, confidence, or credibility.

When my client, let's call her Cara, a coach & consultant
Faced a similar challenge. 

They were negotiating a $20K contract.

The prospect requested:
A 6 month breakout clause in a 12-month agreement.

My client assumed this meant they wanted to split payments.
Half now, half in six months.

The reality? 
They simply wanted the flexibility to leave if needed.

The danger of assuming? It can lead you to:

• Devalue your offer 
• Misinterpret a buyer’s intent.

Instead, we focused on:
Clarifying the ask and leading the conversation.

The result? 
They closed the deal at FULL price.

Key takeaway: 
In sales, assumptions don’t just cost you money
They cost you trust. 

Ask questions. Clarify. 
And always lead with value, not fear.
