Your buyer isn't ignoring your LinkedIn DMs.
 
They're not reading them.
 
One of the biggest mistakes I see coaches and consultants make is assuming senior decision-makers live on LinkedIn.
 
Most don't.
 
They live in:
 
→ their inbox 
→ leadership meetings 
→ board updates 
→ budget reviews
 
And while you're sending your 37th LinkedIn DM...
 
They're making decisions somewhere else.
 
If you sell leadership coaching, executive coaching, or B2B consulting into companies, here's how I'd approach corporate outreach instead:

1. Check for warm paths
Search 1st-degree LinkedIn connections. 
Who do I already know inside that org? 
Ask them for a referral. 
You’d be surprised how many doors are half open.

2. Read what they care about
Go beyond their website, annual report
Search for terms like: 
“Leadership,” “people development,”
This tells me what’s already on fire internally.

3. Use AI to go faster
Pu the company name into ChatGPT or Claude. Ask:
“What are the C-level and board prioritising this year?” 
“Where are they placing their growth bets?”
Saves hours. Gets me insight in minutes.

4. Go deep on Sales Navigator
Filter by job titles in HR, L&D, or exec leadership. 
Add “<1 year in current role” 
To find people making fresh moves. 
New hires = new budgets and openness.

5. Stack my list
Now I’ve got 10–15 decision-makers. Check: 
Who’s a 2nd-degree connection? 
Who has overlap with past clients?
Who’s posted recently? 

6. Ask for the intro properly
I’d prep a short DM for my warm contact. 
Voice note or video for extra stand out
Thought it made sense to reach out given [Y].”
“Who’s best to speak with about [X]? 

7.  Be human + clear
Audio DM > long wall of text. 
I’d also engage with their content with comments. 
Something that shows I understand the context.
If not active, engage with company posts.

8. Post relevant content on LinkedIn
Something that aligns with what I just researched. 

“Why your first 90 days as an exec - retention risk.” 
“3 leadership blind spots hurting your rising talent.”
Educate them on the why of coaching.

Because cold DMs open doors. 
But content makes people trust you.

Now you dont need to send 100s of DMs
Just zone in with deeper messaging
