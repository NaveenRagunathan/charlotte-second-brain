The fastest way to lose a sale is to present your offer too early.
The second fastest?

Getting to the end of the call and never presenting it at all.
That is the mistake a lot of founders make.

They do not know when the buyer is actually ready.

So they either:
→ pitch too soon 
→ keep talking in circles 
→ or miss the moment completely

Here is the rule I use:
The buyer has to earn the right to hear the offer.

ETR.

Because the goal is not to talk about your offer as quickly as possible.

The goal is to talk about it when the buyer is bought in enough to hear it properly.

That means they need to show they are ready to solve the problem.

Not just curious. 
Not just interested. Ready.
Before I present, I test for that with 3 questions:

1. Why does now feel like the right time to invest in solving this?
 
This gets them to sell back to you why the problem matters now.

2. Do you trust this process to help you get the result you want?
 
This tells you whether they believe in the path.

3. Do you trust yourself and me to get this result?
 
This tells you whether they believe in execution.

Those 3 questions change everything.
Because if the answers are weak, the issue is not the offer.

The buyer has not earned the right to hear it yet.

And when that happens, presenting too early usually creates pressure instead of progress.

But when the buyer answers those questions clearly, confidently, and with conviction?

That is usually your cue.
Now the offer makes sense.
Now the timing is right.

Now the conversation can move forward without forcing it.

A lot of founders think closing is about saying the right thing at the end.

Usually, it is about knowing whether the buyer is ready before you get there.

That is the real skill.

Do you have a way to test buyer readiness before you present your offer?
