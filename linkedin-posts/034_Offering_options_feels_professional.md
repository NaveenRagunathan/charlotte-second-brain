Offering options feels professional.
It’s usually what slows the deal down.

This week:
→ $7,200 closed 
→ $3,000 in 3-month engagements 
→ And a separate $60K win 

Nothing changed structurally. 
No new system. 
Better leadership on the call.

Just this:
Stop presenting options. 
Start making recommendations.
The default sounds like this:

“Here are two ways we could do this.” 
“Have a think.” 
“I’ll send something over.”

That’s vendor posture.
One client changed a single sentence.
From: “Here are a couple of ways we could structure it.”

To: “Based on what you’ve shared, here’s what I recommend. 
Six months. $4,200. 

Here’s why. If we want this live by X, next step is Y.”
Decision made on the call.

Premium buyers don’t want a menu.
They want clarity.

When hesitation shows up, don’t collapse into reassurance.

Ask: 
“What exactly needs thinking - price, scope, timing, or trust?”

That question changes the tone immediately.
Advisors guide decisions. Vendors circulate options.

The revenue gap between the two is not subtle.
