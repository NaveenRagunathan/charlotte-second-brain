”Just checking in” is the business version of texting your ex at 2am.
 
It feels desperate. And it kills momentum.
 
Stop sending it. Use these instead:
 
 
1️⃣ Warm them up in public first
 
Drop a short comment on their latest post:
 
“That point about [topic] really landed with me. Curious what made you look at it that way?”
 
Then DM:
 
“Loved your post on [topic], especially [specific bit]. Wanted to circle back on our last convo about [project]…”
 
You’re engaging like a human, not lurking in their inbox.
 
 
2️⃣ Ask permission before you help
 
Instead of dumping advice, try:
 
“Would it be useful if I shared what worked for a client in a similar spot?”
 
You’re not forcing help. You’re offering it and letting them lead.
 
 
3️⃣ Share a relevant win, casually
 
“Quick one: a client in your industry just hit [specific result]. I thought of what you mentioned about [their goal] on our call.”
 
It’s proof without the pitch. You remind them you exist, without being weird about it.
 
 
4️⃣ The actual chill check-in
 
“Hey, still thinking about getting support around [their challenge], or did priorities shift?”
 
No pressure. Be honest, direct.
 
 
5️⃣ Send something genuinely useful
 
Article, template, or post that actually maps to their situation:
 
“Saw this and thought of our conversation about [topic]. Thought it might be helpful.”
 
No strings attached. They feel helped, not hunted.
 
 
6️⃣ Anchor back to 𝘵𝘩𝘦𝘪𝘳 timeline
 
“You mentioned Q4 was when you wanted to tackle [goal]. Does that still feel right?”
 
You’re holding them to what 𝘵𝘩𝘦𝘺 said they wanted, not what 𝘺𝘰𝘶 want to sell.
 
 
7️⃣ Leave the door open 
 
“I’ve got a spot opening next week if now feels like the right time to revisit this. If not, no stress at all.”
 
Follow-up isn’t about pestering. It’s about remembering the details and caring about the outcome.
 
Not copy-pasting “just checking in” to 47 people.
