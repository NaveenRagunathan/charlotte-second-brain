In 2011, I was laughed at and mocked.
Not by strangers. 

By friends. 
By colleagues. 
By people who said they cared.

I told them I was quitting my salaried job. 
Walking away from the "safe paycheck." 
Going commission-only.

Weird huh? 

No base. No backup. I was SO excited by it.
Just me, a phone, a laptop and the acid test of sales.
I worked from home in 2011, before it was "chic"

They said: 

"You're crazy." "That’s risky." 
"Why would you throw away security?"

I didn’t argue. I just got to work.
Water off a duck's back.
And yes, it was hard. Really hard.

The not knowing at first how my month would end
Working at all times of the day 
The first few months it stung.

Yet built the muscle I still use today.

14 years later? 
Those skills closed $100K in January 2025. 
And they’ve put me on track to hit 7 figures by year end.

That decision in 2011? Prepped me for everything I do now: 
Running my own business 
Leading a team 
Helping business owners sell elegantly.

People will always mock what they’d never have the guts to do.

→ They’ll call it risky. 
→ They’ll call it unstable. 
→ They’ll call it crazy.

What they really mean is: 
“I’d never take that bet on myself.”

So if you’re building something bold right now.
Ignore the critics. Just get on with it
They’re not your clients. They’re not your peers. 

They’d never walk the road you’re on.
Let them laugh. You’re too busy building.

You ALL have an elegant salesperson within you.
Never let ANYONE tell you different.
