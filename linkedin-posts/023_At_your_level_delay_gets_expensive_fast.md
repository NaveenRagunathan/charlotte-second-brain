At your level, delay gets expensive fast. 
Not just in time: in lost revenue, traction, and trust.

A lot of experienced founders call it “giving it a bit more time.”
 
Another quarter. 
Another tweak to the offer. 
Another attempt to make the content land.

But the issue is rarely time.
 
It usually means you're still paying for:
 
→ an offer that is not sharp enough 
→ a message that still makes buyers work too hard 
→ a sales process that leaves too much to chance
 
That is where the cost builds.
 
David Sandler built an entire sales system around one brutal question:
 
𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝘀 𝗰𝗼𝘀𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗿𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁 𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝗶𝗳 𝗻𝗼𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗰𝗵𝗮𝗻𝗴𝗲𝘀?
 
Not eventually. Not in theory. Right now.
 
Most founders never sit with that question properly.
 
So the cost stays invisible.
And invisible costs compound.

I see this on founder sales calls all the time.
 
A prospect says: “𝗜’𝗺 𝗻𝗼𝘁 𝘀𝘂𝗿𝗲 𝗶𝘁’𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗿𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁 𝗺𝗼𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁.”
 
And the founder asks: “𝗖𝗮𝗻 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗮𝗳𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗱 𝘁𝗼 𝘄𝗮𝗶𝘁?”
 
Wrong question.
Of course they can survive another month.
 
That was never the point.
The real question is:
 
𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗲𝘅𝗮𝗰𝘁𝗹𝘆 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗯𝗲 𝗱𝗶𝗳𝗳𝗲𝗿𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗶𝗻 𝗳𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘄𝗲𝗲𝗸𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘀 𝗻𝗼𝘁 𝗮𝘃𝗮𝗶𝗹𝗮𝗯𝗹𝗲 𝘁𝗼 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗿𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁 𝗻𝗼𝘄?
 
That is where the conversation gets honest.
 
That is Sandler.
Not pressure. Diagnosis.
 
Because most of the time, the answer is: 𝗻𝗼𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴.
 
And that is the moment, cost of inaction becomes real.
Most founders never get there.
