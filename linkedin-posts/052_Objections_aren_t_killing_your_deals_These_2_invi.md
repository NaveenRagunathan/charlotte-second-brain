Objections aren't killing your deals. These 2 invisible fears are.

I've spent way too much time analyzing buyer psychology studies, and it's almost never about your service.

It's about invisible barriers stopping the decision.

The two that kill most deals?

Trust deficit: 80% of B2B buyers regret their vendor choice. 
Your buyer isn't evaluating you in a vacuum. 
They're carrying baggage from every bad decision before you.

Status quo bias: Even when you're objectively better, change feels risky. 
Better to stick with what they know, even if it's not great.

Most coaches and consultants try to overcome objections.
That's backwards.

Your job isn't to push harder. It's to remove friction.

Show proof early. Make switching painless. 
Address the fear of making the wrong choice before they even voice it.

Look, buyers aren't being difficult for fun. 
They're protecting themselves from regret.
