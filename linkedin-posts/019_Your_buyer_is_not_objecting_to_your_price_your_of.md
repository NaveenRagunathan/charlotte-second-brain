Your buyer is not objecting to your price, your offer, or your timing.
 
They are usually objecting to change itself.
 
I reviewed 30 objections from client sales calls. 
The same pattern kept showing up.

That is the part most founders miss.
 
They hear:
 
“I’m in a good place now.” 
 “Let me do some maths.” 
“I want to try it myself first.” 
 “I just need one more client, then I’ll be ready.”
 
And they treat each one like a separate objection.
 
Most of the time, it is the same pattern underneath.
The buyer is trying to reduce discomfort.
Not solve the problem.
 
That matters.
 
Because if you only respond to the words, you stay at surface level.
 
But if you understand what the objection actually means, you can handle the sale much more cleanly.
 
A few examples:
 
“I’m in a good place now” 
Usually means they have confused comfort with progress.
 
“Let me do some maths and get back to you” 
Usually means they are buying time to talk themselves out of the decision.
 
“I’m going to try and figure it out myself first” 
Usually means they still believe time will solve what strategy has not.
 
“I’ve never hired a coach before” 
Usually means they are scared of making the wrong decision on something intangible.
 
“I just need to land one more client, then I’ll be ready” 
Usually means they are stuck in circular logic.
 
This is why objection handling is not about clever rebuttals.
 
It is about diagnosis.
The words are rarely the problem. 
The pattern underneath is.
 
If you can name what is actually happening, you do not need to convince anyone.
 
You just need to help the buyer see what they have already told you.
