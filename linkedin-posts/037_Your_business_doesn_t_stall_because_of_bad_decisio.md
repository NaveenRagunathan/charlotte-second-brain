Your business doesn’t stall because of bad decisions. 
It stalls because you wait to feel ready.

Most people don’t fail because they invest badly. 
They fail because they wait to feel “ready.”

Annie Duke talks about this in 𝘛𝘩𝘪𝘯𝘬𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘪𝘯 𝘉𝘦𝘵𝘴: 
Waiting for certainty feels responsible 
But it’s often the most expensive decision you make.
 
I used to think that was smart too.
 
Last year, I deployed $47k into my business. 
Now it wasnt to feel confident
It was because standing still was costing me more.
 
The version of me that hesitates over numbers? 
She doesn’t grow.
 
So I use one rule now:
 
If I can’t see a clear 3x return within 6 months, 
I don’t invest.
 
That filter alone killed most opportunities that looked good but wouldn’t actually move the needle.
 
What I invest in now: 

– Systems that buy back time 
– People who compound faster than I do 
– Tools that shorten decision cycles
 
This isn’t about hustle or mindset. 
It’s about knowing what outcome you’re buying.
 
Belief doesn’t come first. Commitment does.
 
So the real question isn’t “Can I afford this?”
 
It’s: What is waiting already costing me?
