An inbound DM is not a lead yet. It is an opening.
 
Most founders treat an inbound message like the hard work is done. Someone reached out. 

They are interested. 
Now just reply and book the call.
 
But inbound DMs fail to convert for the same reason cold outreach does. 

The response was written without thinking about the buyer.
 
Too eager. 
Too much information. 
Too fast to the calendar link. 

Too vague to give the person a reason to take the next step.
 
The conversation dies not because the lead was bad. 
But because the response did not do its job.
 
Claude can help you fix that.

1. Diagnose what the inbound message is really asking Not every DM means what it says on the surface.
 
2. Write a response that qualifies without interrogating Get the information you need without killing the momentum.
 
3. Decide whether this lead is worth pursuing Not every inbound is a good fit. Know that before you invest.
 
4. Move a warm conversation to a booked call naturally The transition from chat to calendar should feel like the obvious next step.
 
5. Respond to a vague inbound without guessing "I'd love to find out more" is not enough to work with. Here is how to handle it.
 
6. Re-engage an inbound that went quiet after your first reply You responded. They disappeared. Here is what to do next.
 
7. Write a response to a high-value inbound where the stakes are higher Some leads deserve a more considered reply. Here is how to write one.
