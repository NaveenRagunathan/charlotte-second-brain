When a buyer says "I need to think about it" or "send me something," they are not saying no.
 
They are telling you exactly where alignment broke down.

1️⃣ “I Need to Think About It”
 
❌ “Of course, take your time.” 
✅ “What specifically needs thinking: risk, timing, budget, or internal buy-in?”
→ Vague objections need specific questions.
 
 
2️⃣ “It’s Too Expensive”
 
❌ Justifying your price 
✅ “Compared to what-doing nothing, hiring internally, or another option?” 
→ Price objections are usually value comparison gaps.
 
 
3️⃣ “Now’s Not the Right Time”
 
❌ “No worries, let’s reconnect later.” 
✅ “What changes between now and then?”
→ If nothing changes, neither will the decision.
 
 
4️⃣ “Send Me Something”
 
❌ 12-slide deck 
✅ “What part do you need clarity on?”
→ Information doesn’t create decisions. Relevance does.
 
 
5️⃣ Silence After the Proposal
 
❌ “Just checking in…” 
✅ “Have you ruled this out, or are we still deciding?”
→ Directness reduces drift.
