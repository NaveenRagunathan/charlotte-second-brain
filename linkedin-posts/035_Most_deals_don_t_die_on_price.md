Here’s where most sales conversations collapse:

1️⃣ Seeking Permission to Lead
❌ “Does that sound okay?” 
✅ “What I’d recommend based on what you shared.”
→ Confidence doesn’t ask to exist. It explains its reasoning.

2️⃣ Softening Your Own Value
❌ “This might be a good fit…” 
✅ “This fits because of X, Y, and Z.”
→ “Might” tells buyers you’re unsure before they are.

3️⃣ Filling Silence Too Fast
❌ “Just to add…” 
✅ 𝘚𝘪𝘭𝘦𝘯𝘤𝘦
→ Calm sellers let space do the work. 
Rushing = nervous energy.

4️⃣ Treating Hesitation Like Rejection
❌ “Totally understand if now’s not the right time” 
✅ “What specifically would you need to see to move forward?”
→ Hesitation is data, not a dead end.

5️⃣ Explaining Instead of Orienting
❌ “Let me walk you through everything” 
✅ “Here’s the part that matters most for your situation”
→ Buyers want relevance, not volume.

6️⃣ Asking Questions Without Direction
❌ “Any thoughts?” 
✅ “What’s your biggest consideration right now?”
→ Vague questions invite vague answers.

7️⃣ Leaving the Decision Floating
❌ “Let me know what you think” 
✅ “What’s the next step on your side?”
→ Deals stall when responsibility is unclear.
