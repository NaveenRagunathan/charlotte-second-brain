I didn't leave corporate when my side hustle replaced my salary.
 
I left when staying felt riskier than leaving.
 
Everyone tells you to wait until your business replaces 100% of your income.
 
That sounds sensible.
 
It also keeps a lot of talented people stuck for years.
 
In my case, the decision became obvious when I closed a deal worth more than my annual basic salary.
 
Not my OTE.
 
Not my commission.
 
My entire yearly basic salary.
 
That deal changed how I saw myself.
 
Because suddenly the question wasn't:
 
"𝘊𝘢𝘯 𝘵𝘩𝘪𝘴 𝘣𝘶𝘴𝘪𝘯𝘦𝘴𝘴 𝘸𝘰𝘳𝘬?"
 
It was:
 
"𝘏𝘰𝘸 𝘮𝘶𝘤𝘩 𝘭𝘰𝘯𝘨𝘦𝘳 𝘢𝘮 𝘐 𝘨𝘰𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘵𝘰 𝘱𝘳𝘦𝘵𝘦𝘯𝘥 𝘪𝘵 𝘤𝘢𝘯'𝘵?"

I was already proving I could create value outside the company.
I was already generating opportunities.
I was already building momentum.

Could I have waited until my business replaced every penny of my corporate income?

Of course.

It would have felt safer.
Sometimes "safe" is just leaving the band aid on.

I think founders ask the wrong question.
Instead of:

"𝘞𝘩𝘦𝘯 𝘴𝘩𝘰𝘶𝘭𝘥 𝘐 𝘭𝘦𝘢𝘷𝘦?"

Ask:

"𝘈𝘮 𝘐 𝘴𝘦𝘦𝘪𝘯𝘨 𝘦𝘯𝘰𝘶𝘨𝘩 𝘦𝘷𝘪𝘥𝘦𝘯𝘤𝘦 𝘵𝘩𝘢𝘵 𝘐 𝘤𝘢𝘯 𝘣𝘶𝘪𝘭𝘥 𝘵𝘩𝘪𝘴 𝘸𝘪𝘵𝘩𝘰𝘶𝘵 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘴𝘢𝘧𝘦𝘵𝘺 𝘯𝘦𝘵?"

Because there comes a point where the business is not the experiment anymore.

The job is.

And if you're consistently:

→ attracting clients 
→ closing deals 
→ generating demand 
→ building confidence in your ability to sell

The biggest leap wasn't financial.
 
It was believing I could create opportunities without someone else's logo behind me.

At some point, you stop needing certainty.
And start trusting your ability to figure it out.

When did you realise your business might work?
