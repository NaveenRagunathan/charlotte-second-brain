The most expensive decision in business is usually "not yet."

A leaking roof is one of the best lessons you'll ever learn.
 
Imagine water starts dripping through your ceiling.
 
A roofer tells you:
 
"𝘐 𝘤𝘢𝘯 𝘧𝘪𝘹 𝘪𝘵 𝘵𝘰𝘥𝘢𝘺 𝘧𝘰𝘳 $500."
 
You decide to wait.
$500 feels annoying.
 
Life gets busy.
Other things feel more urgent.
So you leave it.
 
Three months later:
 
The leak has spread.
The ceiling is damaged.
The walls are damaged.
The flooring is damaged.
 
Now the bill is $3,500.
 
A $500 problem became a $3,500 problem.
 
Same leak.
 
7x the cost.
 
This is what most buyers miss.
 
The cost of a problem doesn't pause while they decide.
 
It compounds.
 
Every week a founder delays fixing:
 
→ weak positioning 
→ inconsistent lead flow 
→ poor conversion 
→ lack of visibility
 
The cost gets bigger.
 
The solution didn't change. 
The situation and consequences did.
 
The best sales conversations I've ever had weren't about convincing someone.
 
They were about helping them see what waiting was costing them.
 
Most people focus on the fix.
 
The better question is:
 
𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗵𝗮𝗽𝗽𝗲𝗻𝘀 𝗶𝗳 𝗻𝗼𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗰𝗵𝗮𝗻𝗴𝗲𝘀?
 
That's usually where the real urgency lives.

Sell the water damage, not the roof repair.
