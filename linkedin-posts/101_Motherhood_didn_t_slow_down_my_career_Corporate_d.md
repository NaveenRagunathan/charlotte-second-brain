Motherhood didn’t slow down my career. Corporate did.

When my twins were babies, I was on planes twice a month.

Closing 6 figure deals.

Juggling client meetings in one timezone.
Bedtime stories in another on Facetime.

But I always knew something was off.

I had the career.

The titles.
The travel.
But not the freedom.

Then the pandemic hit.

My kids were 4.

And I made a decision:
To build a business that worked for my life,
Not someone else’s bottom line.

So I started from scratch.

No website.
A 3k audience.
Just a deep knowledge of sales.

And the drive to do things differently.

Fast-forward to now:

My twins are 9.
I run a successful business fully online.
I help experienced coaches and consultants sell with clarity, confidence, and calm.

And I still make the school run.

A growing team.
No awkward cold DMs.
No burnout.

Just strategy, systems, and a sales process that actually works.

Motherhood didn’t hold me back. 
It focused me.

Because when your time is limited.
Your priorities become sharp.

I do in 60 minutes what would've taken me 3 hours.

And when you stop building for someone else’s goals, you finally make space for your own.

Building your business while raising kids?

You’re not behind.
You’re building on your terms.
And that’s the real power move.

PS. On a boat in Bur Dubai.
