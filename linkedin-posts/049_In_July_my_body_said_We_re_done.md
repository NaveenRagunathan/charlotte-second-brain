In July, my body said: “We’re done.”
Perimenopause hit hard. 

Burnout wasn’t a warning. It forced a reset.
What made it harder to admit?

The business was actually doing really well.
Clients. Momentum. Visibility.
 
From the outside, everything looked like it was working.

The business was running almost entirely on my energy. 
And once that started to drain, the whole thing wobbled.

That moment forced a truth most leaders don’t like to say out loud:

You can have the strategy. 
The clients. The growth.
And still be building something that isn’t sustainable.

Here’s what I’m taking into 2026, learned the hard way:

𝟭. 𝗬𝗼𝘂’𝗿𝗲 𝗼𝗻𝗹𝘆 𝗮𝘀 𝗴𝗼𝗼𝗱 𝗮𝘀 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗲𝗻𝗲𝗿𝗴𝘆 
Not your hustle. 
Not your hours. 
Your actual, sustainable capacity. 
Big numbers don’t matter if you’re running on fumes.

𝟮. 𝗠𝗶𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗼𝗻 𝗯𝗲𝗮𝘁𝘀 𝗺𝗲𝘁𝗿𝗶𝗰𝘀 
I started asking better questions: 
Is this aligned with my life? 
Am I serving the right people? 
Does this still matter to me?

𝟯. 𝗦𝗮𝗹𝗲𝘀 𝗶𝘀 𝗻𝗼𝗻-𝗻𝗲𝗴𝗼𝘁𝗶𝗮𝗯𝗹𝗲 
Even when you’re tired. 
Even when momentum is there. 
Revenue gives you options. 
Never deprioritize the skill that keeps you in control.

𝟰. 𝗗𝗶𝘀𝗰𝗼𝗻𝗻𝗲𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 𝗶𝘀 𝗽𝗮𝗿𝘁 𝗼𝗳 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗷𝗼𝗯 
The more “on” I tried to be
The less effective I became. 
Stepping away didn’t slow the business down. 
It made my decisions better.

𝟱. 𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗯𝗼𝗱𝘆 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗰𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗹𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗼𝗻 𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗻𝘁𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 
Burnout isn’t random. It’s feedback.
A business can’t run on your energy alone. 
Systems, support, and boundaries aren’t luxuries. They’re survival.

I’ve got big plans for 2026.

But this time, I’m building from a place that can hold them.
