If you're not going to buy, don't say yes.
A fake yes is not polite.
It's cowardly.

You're not sparing someone's feelings. 
You're protecting yourself and handing the cost to them.

When you say, “I’m in. Let’s do it.” then disappear,
you do not leave things neutral.

A founder starts planning around you. 
Capacity gets mentally allocated. 
Time gets moved. 

Attention gets pulled off real opportunities.

People start acting on a decision you never actually made.
That is not a small social mistake.
That is you making your discomfort someone else's problem.
 
A real no lets adults make decisions. 
A real no protects time, trust, and momentum.
A fake yes does the opposite.

It burns time. 
It dirties the pipeline. 
It makes people doubt your word.
And leaders should take this personally too.

If people keep telling you yes and backing out later, look at the environment you built.
Some leaders punish honesty. Some sellers corner people into agreement. Some teams make it easier to nod now and tell the truth later.
Same result.

People lie early because the room made honesty expensive.
That is how bad deals get into the forecast. 
That is how teams chase work that was never real. 
That is how trust gets chipped away before the contract even starts.

If you're selling, stop grabbing for the yes.

Do not treat hesitation like commitment. 
Do not mistake politeness for intent. 
Do not leave calls feeling good when the other person left feeling trapped.

Your job is not to get agreement. 
Your job is to get the truth.
Because a deal built on pressure usually dies in silence.

And if this hits a nerve, sit with that.

A lot of people call themselves nice when they're really just avoiding a hard sentence.

Adults say no.
Children say yes and hope it goes away.

Respect is telling the truth before someone else pays for your lie.
