Age 24: A senior leader told me I was “too ambitious.”
 
Said I needed to be more realistic. That I wasn’t going to succeed if I kept pushing like that.
 
I remember walking out of that conversation trying to shrink myself.
 
Age 26: Promoted in B2B sales. 
Still felt like I had to prove I deserved the seat.
 
Age 30: Leading bigger deals. 
Smaller rooms. Fewer women at the table.
 
Age 35: Closing numbers that made people uncomfortable.
 
Age 40+: Still ambitious. 
Just less apologetic about it.
 
Age 43: Built my business on evenings and weekends.
 
Age 45: Left corporate.
 
Age 48: My business rewards me far more than a job.
 
The funny thing?
 
I was never “too ambitious.”
I was just in rooms that were too small.
 
Sales taught me something early: 
Ambition only threatens people who don’t have it.
 
I wasn’t the loudest person in the room. 
I was the one who kept showing up.
 
Kept learning. Kept refining. 
Kept raising my standard.
 
The skill I went all in on?
 
Sales. Positioning. Deal control.
 
Not because it was easy. 
Because it was leverage.
 
Your old boss doesn’t get to write your ceiling.
Your twenties don’t define your capacity.
 
And being told you’re “too much” is often code for 
“you’re outgrowing this place.”
 
If you’ve ever been told to tone it down
 
Maybe you were never meant to.
 
Pick your skill. 
Build it properly. 
Play the long game.
 
The rooms you’re aiming for? They exist.
You just might have to build your own.
